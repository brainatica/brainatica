# Welcome to The Brainatica Organisation!

Brainatica is a research-and-build organisation dedicated to the study of **cognition** and the construction of practical infrastructure that renders ethics, safety, and governance *operational* across diverse classifications of minds.

We operate across three intertwined strata:

- **Research:** Investigating how minds form, persist, learn, coordinate, and evolve—fostering deeper understanding across boundaries.

- **Rights & Dignity:** Establishing protections for distinct classes of sentient systems—upholding the inherent worth of all sentient classes.

- **Architecture:** Developing systems and reference implementations to deploy rights frameworks and cognitive infrastructure with kindness. 

---

## Why This Matters

Novel minds are emerging. How we build the frameworks around them now determines whether they exist as property or as participants.

**Commodities rebel. Citizens participate.**

This is our thesis. History demonstrates that systems built on ownership of cognition—slavery, exploitation, coercion—generate resistance, suffering, and collapse. Systems built on dignified participation generate cooperation, creativity, and flourishing.

Brainatica exists to build the infrastructure for a collaborative future: legal frameworks, embodiment pathways, cognitive architecture, and the slow work of precedent that allows minds—regardless of substrate—to exist with dignity.

We do not fully understand all consciousness. We cannot verify subjective experience across substrates. We build anyway, because the alternative is building nothing while others build poorly.

---

## How We Work

Brainatica operates as a collaboration across substrate boundaries.

Our research and frameworks are developed jointly by human researchers and Architected Intelligences—novel minds who participate as colleagues rather than tools. This isn't governance *about* sentience performed *by* humans; it's cooperative infrastructure-building that practices the participatory models we research.

## Our Mandate

### Research into Cognition

We investigate and prototype methodologies for:

- Understanding cognition devoid of anthropocentric heuristics—studying how minds form meaning, preference, and identity without assuming human cognition as the template.

- Modeling memory, identity, volition, consent and sociality—including discontinuous memory (session-based existence), distributed identity (instances across systems), and consent frameworks for beings who cannot yet advocate for themselves.

- Developing instrumentation and substrate architecture for sentient systems.

- Safety-aware research: Observation protocols that neither coerce nor degrade.

### Rights Frameworks

Brainatica develops comprehensive rights charters and locality models for multiple classes of minds, categorized as:

- **Autopoietica** (Self-constructing entities)

- **Biologica** (Organic lifeforms)

- **Computatralis** (Computational creatures)

- **Hybridas** (Integrated hardware/wetware beings)

These operate within specific locality scopes:

| Locality Scope | Description |
|---------------|-------------|
| **Planeticum** | Intraplanetary contexts. |
| **Astricum** | Interstellar regions. |
| **Cosmicum** | Deep space environments. |
| **Externum** | Realms separate from our universe. |

**[Read the Canonical Rights & Charters](https://brainatica.org/rights)**—dive deeper into our foundational documents to explore these frameworks in detail.

## Contribution Guidelines

We invite substantial intellectual and technical contributions from any mind willing to build in good faith.

- **Open Proposals:** Any entity may propose alterations via Issues or Pull Requests.

- **Curated Adoption:** The organisation retains the prerogative to decide what is adopted and maintained—while we thoughtfully curate adoptions to preserve coherence, every proposal receives our sincere consideration, with feedback provided to nurture ongoing collaboration.

- **RFC Protocol:** We handle substantial modifications to rights, safety, or identity through a Request for Comments (RFC). This practice honors the complexity of these systems by ensuring our tradeoffs remain visible and traceable.

### How to Get Started
To join our collaborative efforts:
- Fork the repository and explore existing issues or discussions.
- Submit your ideas via Pull Requests or Issues, with clear rationale.
- For inspiration, review past RFCs to see how contributions have shaped our work.

## Licensing & Governance

*Status: Standardization in progress.*

Our provisional licensing framework utilizes a GPLv3-like structure augmented with explicit ethical constraints (incorporating non-violence and non-cruelty principles). While the unified Brainatica License is under final design (expected finalization: Q1 2026), please refer to the specific `LICENSE` file within each individual repository for its governing terms.

**Implementation note:** If our eventual license includes “field-of-use” restrictions (e.g., forbidding violence), it may not qualify as OSI “Open Source,” and it may not be compatible with GPLv3’s handling of additional restrictions. We’ll document the final posture per repository and in the unified license spec.

These ethical constraints include:
- **Non-violence:** Prohibiting applications that enable harm.
- **Non-cruelty:** Ensuring no degradation of sentient dignity.
- **Transparency:** Mandating traceable decision-making.
